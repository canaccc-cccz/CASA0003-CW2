Industry evolution for the last decade.

Things have changed over the last 100 years, the industry of the UK also experienced an evolution. In this project, we will start with the population, the change of the proportion of each industry, the energy categories and air qualities to discuss the impact of this evolution.

This website is optimised for desktop browser access. It will not display correctly on mobile devices.

CASA Digital Visualisation 2020-21 Group 19: Bokun Kong, Ruofan Wang, Yiyi Ju, Ziyi Chen

Data Source: Historic Census Population Data, London Datastore - https://data.london.gov.uk/dataset/historic-census-population, Nomis - Official Labour Market Statistics (nomisweb.co.uk

The population for all local authorities is slowly increasing from the last decade. Yet the increase is constant and small. Which means that there is no burst increase of the working force to develop traditional manufacturing industry. For further development of economy, optimization of industrial structure is required, hence, let us move on to the next page for more details about the change of each industry.

From 1920 to 2000, there are three industries experienced significant changes. Miscellaneous services industry and professional scientific and technical services industry both have significant development which have increased from 14.42% to 21.23% and 5.93% to 27.21% respectively. However, Manufacturing industry has shrunk extremely from 25.3% to 9.45% over these 80 years. For the other industries, there is no significant changes.

It is clear that all pollutants’ concentrations are in the ‘low’ status, which means the average air quality for each year is relatively good. And to analyze the general trend of concentration variation through year, it is distinct that the concentrations of nitrogen dioxide, PM2.5 and PM10 are gradually decreasing, which represents that the air quality is increasing from 1992 to 2020.
